About Me

I am currently a doctoral candidate in Computer Engineering at Northwestern University. I have a B.E. (Bachelor of Engineering) in Chemical Engineering and M.Eng. in Software Systems from BITS Pilani, India and a M.S. in Computer Science from Northwestern University. Hopefully by 2018, I would be having 4 degrees in 4 different disciplines.

Also, I am very curious about knowing the world, and have an eclectic taste.

I also love reading about psychology and philosophy. Although not a social scientist, the complexity of the mind have always interested me. Although it might sound cliched, I had dreamt of being a researcher or a scientist since I was 6 years old. So I am grateful to all the teachers and mentors and my friends in my life for where I am. I remember reading about quantum and nuclear physics in my middle school. I feel proud to be born in the land of Aryabhatta, Ramanujan and Bose.

During my time at Northwestern, I have worked in different labs before finally finding my passion. I have worked on internet privacy in the Networks Lab led by Prof. Kuzmanovic, and social media at the Northwestern Social Media Lab (SML) directed by Prof. Birnholtz.

Right now, I am working at the CUCIS lab led by Prof. Alok Choudhary. I am working on materials informatics and materials data analytics.

My current hobbies are writing and public speaking. I am the Treasurer of the Northwestern Toastmasters Club. I am also the President of the Northwestern Cricket Club.

I am a Segal Design Institute Fellow, and received the Segal Fellowship at the beginning of my 3rd year of graduate school. The fellowship is for promising students I am currently finishing up completing a paper on my work during that time.

I had been fortunate to be supported by my parents and my sister who have always encouraged me to study, even when our family were in financial crisis.

Teaching is one of my passions. I enjoy spending time with my students while TAing and whenever I take a course, I imagine how I would like to teach the same course. I do see myself as a professor 10 yrs down the line. But, I would like to work in industrial research for a while. Right now, their is a lot of pioneering research in Machine Learning happening in industry. Google DeepMind, FAIR, IBM Watson, MSR are currently what Bell Labs were in the 1960s.

I have often felt that the academic environment often disadvantages grad students especially internationals. And, one of my future goals is to become a spokesmen for the grad students when I would have the power to influence the academic system.
